Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Neo Social Autopilot handles information used in creator publishing workflows.

1. Information Used by the Tool

Neo Social Autopilot may process or store the following information:

  • Selected video file paths
  • Video titles
  • Captions
  • Hashtags
  • Scheduled publishing times
  • Publishing queue data
  • Post history and upload results
  • Platform response data, such as upload status or post IDs
  • OAuth access or refresh tokens for connected creator accounts

2. How Information Is Used

Information is used to prepare posts, schedule posts, upload selected content, display publishing results, refresh authorized platform connections, and help users troubleshoot failed uploads.

3. Connected Platform Authorization

Neo Social Autopilot uses official authorization methods where available. For TikTok, the tool uses TikTok Login Kit to let users authorize a connected TikTok account. Posting actions use TikTok’s Content Posting API when approved and authorized.

4. Local Storage

Neo Social Autopilot is a desktop workflow tool. Queue data, history data, selected file paths, and OAuth tokens may be stored locally on the user’s computer so the publishing workflow can operate reliably.

5. Third-Party Platforms

When a user chooses to publish content to a third-party platform, relevant video files, titles, captions, hashtags, account authorization data, and upload metadata may be sent to that platform through its official API.

Third-party platforms process submitted content according to their own privacy policies, terms, API rules, and community guidelines.

6. No Sale of Personal Data

Neo Social Autopilot does not sell personal data.

7. Data Removal

Users can remove locally stored queue, history, upload, and token files from their computer. Users may also revoke connected platform access through the relevant platform’s account settings.
